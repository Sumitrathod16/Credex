# GTM.md — SpendLens Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The exact person is:

**Engineering Manager or CTO at a Series A/seed startup (10–80 employees), using 3+ AI tools across the team, who approved the AI tool budget 6–12 months ago and has never re-evaluated it.**

More specifically:
- Title: CTO, VP Engineering, Engineering Manager, or Head of Product at a B2B SaaS startup
- Company: 10–80 employees, raised seed to Series A ($500k–$10M)
- Situation: Added Cursor for devs, Claude for content, ChatGPT for customer ops — one at a time, never audited holistically
- Pain: Monthly AI bills are $2k–$8k and climbing. No one owns the category. Finance asks questions they can't answer.
- Trigger event: Budget review, board meeting prep, or a Slack message from finance asking "what are we spending on AI?"

This person Googles: "how much should a startup spend on AI tools" or "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot cost comparison" or "reduce AI tool costs startup."

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1: Seeding with value, not marketing**

1. **Post on HN as "Show HN: SpendLens — free AI spend audit for startups."** Target Tuesday 9am PT (highest engagement). The headline: "I audited 15 startup AI stacks and found an average of $800/mo wasted. Built a free tool to automate it." Link real (anonymized) examples.

2. **Post the audit method as a Twitter thread** — "How to audit your AI stack in 10 minutes (most startups overpay by 30%)." End with: "I built a free tool that does this automatically." No link until tweet 8/10 — let the content earn it.

3. **DM 20 IndieHackers** who've posted about cutting SaaS costs in the last 60 days. Not a pitch: "Hey, I saw your post about Notion pricing. Built a free AI spend audit tool — would love a sanity check from someone who's been through this." 5/20 will try it.

**Week 2: Community embeds**

4. **Post in r/ExperiencedDevs:** "We audited 50 startup AI stacks. Here's what we found." Data-first post, SpendLens as the tool we used. Genuinely helpful.

5. **Reply to every tweet/post where someone mentions "Cursor pricing" or "ChatGPT cost" in the last 7 days.** Not spam — just: "we built a free tool that benchmarks this. no login needed." This requires 30 minutes of daily social monitoring.

6. **Ping 10 startup founders in the Credex network** (Credex already has relationships). Ask them to run an audit and share the result URL on Twitter. A single retweet from a 10k+ founder drives 200–400 visits.

**Week 3–4: Leverage the shareable URL**

7. **The share URL is the viral loop.** When a user finds $800/mo in savings, they share it. Design the share card to be screenshot-worthy (big number, tool list). Every shared result = organic impressions.

8. **Write a short blog post on the Credex site:** "The 5 most common AI tool overspend patterns we've found." Distribute to Credex email list if any, and pitch to SaaStr blog as a guest post.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already sells AI credits to startups. Every existing Credex customer who used SpendLens would see their own savings — and the Credex CTA appears for >$500/mo cases. This makes SpendLens a **conversion tool within the existing customer base**, not just cold acquisition. No one else can do this because no one else has both the audit tool and the credit supply.

Additionally, Credex can offer **referral incentives**: "Share your audit URL and get a 5% credit on your first Credex purchase." This creates a referral loop that a standalone audit tool can't replicate.
